Hi,
Thank you for agreeing to participate in this user study. The goal of the study is to identify which emotions are expressed in images. To this end, you will see a collection of 300 images and you will be asked to associate each image with one or more of eight basic emotion categories, by clicking at the corresponding checkboxes.

When you finish the study you should click the button saying 'Submit your votes'. Then your submission will be validated and your votes saved.

    Note that:

  1. You should not refresh or close the page during the study.

  2. Use Firefox or Chrome web browsers. The study may also work with other browsers but has not been tested.

  3. You should select at least one emotion category per image. If you are uncertain of what option to choose, please select 'Neutral'.

  4. For each image, please select the emotion (or emotions) that you feel it expresses. Note that this is not necessarily how the content of the image makes you feel, e.g., a crying baby might make you laugh because you find it funny but you should probably select "Sadness" and/or "Fear" if these emotions are conveyed by the image. Also, an image does not have to physically show an emotion, e.g., a dark autumn image may convey the emotion of "Sadness" whereas a bright image taken in summer/spring may convey the emotion of "Joy".

  5. Finally, we kindly ask you not to leave your survey incomplete.

    Definition of emotions;

  1. Anger: is an emotion related to one's psychological interpretation of having been offended, wronged, or denied and a tendency to react through retaliation

  2. Anticipation: or being enthusiastic, is an emotion involving pleasure, excitement, and sometimes anxiety in considering some expected or longed-for good event.

  3. Joy: is a mental or emotional state of well-being characterized by positive or pleasant emotions ranging from contentment to intense joy.

  4. Trust: is believing that the person who is trusted will do what is expected. It starts at the family and grows to others.

  5. Fear: is an emotion induced by a perceived threat which causes entities to quickly pull far away from it and usually hide. It is a basic survival mechanism occurring in response to a specific stimulus, such as pain or the threat of danger.

  6. Surprise: is a brief mental and physiological state, a startle response experienced animals and humans as the result of an unexpected event.

  7. Sadness: is emotional pain associated with, or characterized by feelings of disadvantage, loss, despair, helplessness and sorrow. An individual experiencing sadness may become quiet or lethargic, and withdraw themselves from others. Crying is often an indication of sadness.

  8. Disgust: is a type of aversive reaction that involves withdrawing from a being or object with strong expressions of revulsion. It can also be defined as a revulsion response towards potential contamination.
